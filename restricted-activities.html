Restricted Activities

Version 1.0 — Effective as of March 2026

Bloquo expressly prohibits the use of the Platform, the website bloquo.io, and any Services for the purposes listed below. Violations may result in immediate suspension or termination of access, reporting to competent authorities, and civil and criminal liability.

1. Illicit Activities and Financial Crimes

  • Money laundering, concealment, or disguising the origin, nature, location, or ownership of assets of illicit origin (Law No. 9,613/1998);
  • Financing of terrorism or financing of the proliferation of weapons of mass destruction (Law No. 13,260/2016);
  • Active or passive corruption, bribery, influence peddling, or any acts of misconduct;
  • Tax evasion, tax fraud, or illegal foreign exchange operations;
  • Fraud, embezzlement, or falsification of documents or identities.

2. Transactions with Sanctioned or Blocked Parties

  • Conducting transactions involving individuals, legal entities, or entities subject to international sanctions imposed by the UN Security Council, OFAC (USA), Central Bank of Brazil, COAF, or a competent judicial authority;
  • Circumventing or attempting to circumvent sanctions regimes through intermediary structures, third parties, or alternative jurisdictions;
  • Transacting on behalf of politically exposed persons (PEPs) without the required enhanced due diligence (EDD) process.

3. Platform and Website Misuse

  • Accessing or using the Platform in violation of any applicable federal, state, municipal, or international law, including regulations on data or software export;
  • Inserting, transmitting, or making available to the Platform any malicious code, virus, malware, ransomware, or any harmful technology ("Harmful Code");
  • Overloading, damaging, or impairing the availability of the Platform, including denial-of-service attacks (DoS/DDoS);
  • Using bots, spiders, scrapers, or automated processes to monitor, copy, or extract content from the Platform without prior written authorization;
  • Attempting unauthorized access to any part of the Platform, its servers, databases, or connected systems;
  • Interfering with the proper functioning of the Platform or its implemented security mechanisms.

4. Activities Incompatible with Compliance Policies

  • Use of the Platform by clients classified in high-risk categories not approved under Bloquo's AML/CFT/CPF policies;
  • Conducting transactions with virtual assets obtained from illegitimate sources, including darknet markets, ransomware, phishing, or theft;
  • Structuring or layering transactions to circumvent operational limits, identification requirements, or reporting obligations;
  • Providing false, incomplete, or outdated information during registration, KYC/KYB, foreign exchange transaction classification, or any other compliance step;
  • Transactions involving mixed/tumbled virtual assets without proof of lawful origin.

5. Specific Prohibited Activities for Foreign Exchange and PSAV

  • Processing virtual asset purchase or sale transactions with payment or receipt in foreign currency outside the limits established by the Central Bank of Brazil;
  • Accepting payments in cash, check, physical assets, or any means not authorized by current regulations;
  • Incorrectly classifying the nature or purpose of foreign exchange transactions according to the Central Bank of Brazil categories;
  • Transactions on behalf of third parties without a formal mandate and registration with Bloquo (Authorized Operators).

6. Intellectual Property Violations and Third-Party Rights

  • Reproducing, distributing, modifying, or commercially exploiting Platform content without authorization;
  • Reverse engineering, decompiling, or any attempt to obtain the Platform's source code;
  • Using Bloquo's trademarks, logos, or distinctive signs without prior written authorization.

Consequences

Non-compliance with any item of this policy may result in: immediate suspension or termination of access; preventive asset freezing; reporting to the Central Bank of Brazil, COAF, Federal Revenue Service, FinCEN, and other competent authorities; and civil and criminal liability under applicable law.
